For a long time I kept asking myself an uncomfortable question: do I want a personal site because I truly believe in having a space of my own, or is it an elegant self-deception to avoid exposing myself on social media?

The answer turned out to be simpler than I thought.

## Publishing is not the same as existing on the internet

Social media puts you in a public square where anyone can walk by, judge you, or ignore you. A personal site is something else: it's inviting someone into your home. Whoever arrives, arrives because they meant to.

I want depth before reach. I want what I write to be mine, organized, with no algorithm deciding whether it "performs". I'd rather be found than go out shouting for attention.

That's not cowardice. It's a philosophy of presence.

## The real risk

But I also know when a site like this becomes a lie: when you publish and never tell anyone it exists. When "control" is the excuse to avoid any kind of reaction. When deep down you want connection and the site doesn't create it because nobody sees it.

I don't want to be a **hidden flame**. A flame nobody sees warms no one — and that's not modesty, it's waste.

## The house and the street

So here's the plan: this site is the house. This is where my philosophy lives, my projects, my developed opinions. The things that last. Social media will be the street — not to live there, but so people can find their way here.

And the community, if it comes, will emerge from real content: people who think alike finding me because I said something true. Not because an algorithm pushed them.

This is the first entry. The flame is lit and in plain sight.

Welcome to my house.
